# Direction Calibration

Use explicit axes to avoid accidental defaults. Values are relative design intent, not universal scores.

| Axis | Restrained end | Expressive end | Decision evidence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Composition risk | familiar grid and predictable sequence | asymmetric, layered, or editorial composition | product task, content stability, brand appetite, implementation tolerance |
| Information density | focused, spacious, progressive | compact, comparative, operational | task frequency, scan needs, viewport, error cost |
| Brand expression | product-neutral and conventional | distinctive voice, type, imagery, material | brand assets, trust context, differentiation need |
| Motion prominence | state feedback and subtle continuity | choreographed transitions and narrative movement | task urgency, device/performance, vestibular concerns, reduced-motion path |
| Surface materiality | flat semantic layers | texture, depth, image, light, or dimensional cues | brand/world, readability, asset budget, runtime cost |
| Interaction novelty | native/familiar controls | custom but learnable interaction model | frequency, discoverability, accessibility, platform conventions |
| Content voice | direct/utilitarian | editorial, playful, or cinematic | audience, risk, localization, legal/compliance |
| System rigidity | strict reusable primitives | art-directed exceptions | product scale, team, page uniqueness, maintenance horizon |

## Direction card

```text
Name:
Product rationale:
Axis positions and reasons:
Signature hierarchy/composition:
Typography/content:
Color/material/image:
Motion/interaction:
Responsive transformation:
Accessibility constraints:
Implementation/performance risk:
Use for:
Avoid for:
```

## Calibration rules

- High expression does not require high density, excessive motion, or low contrast.
- Low composition risk does not justify generic card repetition.
- Custom interaction requires a familiar semantic and keyboard/touch path.
- Art-directed exceptions need explicit boundaries so they do not corrupt the component system.
- Calibrate from the brief; never carry one project's values into another automatically.
